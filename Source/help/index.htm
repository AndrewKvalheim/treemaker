Welcome to TreeMaker!

TreeMaker is a tool for origami design. Starting from a description of a desired origami model, TreeMaker computes a crease pattern for folding a base for the model from an uncut square of paper. This documentation describes TreeMaker, how you use it to design origami, and how it works.

The documentation is divided up into several sections. Each section can be printed individually if you'd like to have a hard copy. They are:

  • Background --- gives a little history of this program and the art of origami.
  • Overview --- gives a short overview, which should be enough to get you started if you don't want to read through the entire documentation.
  • Windows --- describes the five major windows in TreeMaker and what they're used for.
  • Menus --- describes the menus and their commands.
  • Tutorials --- contains several tutorials that illustrate how TreeMaker can be used to design origami bases.
    • Tutorial 1: Designing a Base --- Works through the design and construction of a simple base and its crease pattern.
    • Tutorial 2: Imposing Symmetry --- Shows how to create a base with mirror symmetry.
    • Tutorial 3: Working with Strain --- Shows how to vary the length of tree edges to satisfy conditions that give cleaner, more symmetric bases.
  • Tips --- contains advanced tips and techniques for improving your TreeMaker designs and/or making them more foldable.
    • Tips 1: Adding Nodes --- Shows different techniques for adding nodes to a tree.
    • Tips 2: Making a Plan View Design --- Shows how to make a plan view base, i.e., a base that opens out flat.
    • Tips 3: Forcing Edge Flaps --- Shows how and why one might force flaps to lie on the edge or corner of the paper.
    • Tips 4: Fracturing Polygons --- Shows how and why one might wish to break up larger polygons in the crease pattern into smaller patterns.
    • Tips 5: Forcing Symmetric Angles --- Shows how one can force all axial creases to run at symmetric angles for easier folding.

Resources

While this documentation will show you what TreeMaker does, this is not the place to learn about origami design --- a rapidly-growing field that has been revolutionized by the development of mathematical foundations and algorithms over the past two decades. A few sources of information are as follows:

  • For a quick introduction (as well as many examples of origami figures designed with the aid of TreeMaker), check out my website, http://www.langorigami.com, which contains articles about origami, math, and science, and links to other resources on the web.
  • For a more extensive description of origami tree theory and how to design origami, check out my book, Origami Design Secrets (2003: A K Peters Ltd.), and references therein. In particular, it contains many of the equations and algorithms underlying TreeMaker along with extensive discussion of origami designs.
